What is Emergency Lock Opening?

Emergency door opening refers to a series of technical actions performed by a skilled specialist. Their goal is to unlock a client's locks without damaging them, or with minimal damage to the lock's secret part, preventing the destruction of door hardware or overlays on the door leaf.

To successfully unlock doors, a master must have thorough knowledge of the mechanical features of a huge number of lock models available on the modern market. They also need years of experience opening them without damage using specialized tools. If a lock is faulty, they must be able to carefully drill out its secret part to avoid damaging the door leaf and lock hardware.

During emergency door opening in the City two main approaches are used: opening locks without damage and with minimal damage to the lock's coding part. Each approach has its own specifics and consequences. The method chosen depends on the technical condition of the locking mechanisms, the master's qualifications, and the property owner's preferences for how the locks are opened.

Non-Damaging Lock Opening

Provided the lock is in working order, the vast majority — up to 90% of lock mechanisms used on residential doors can be opened without damage using specialized tools, preserving their functionality. The main factors for success are the master's high qualification and specialized unlocking equipment.

When your key is inserted into a lock, it simultaneously sets all coded secrets into the correct position. If the key is lost or broken, a lock opening service specialist uses special tools to mimic the key's action. Relying on their experience and "feel" in their fingers, the master sets each coded element of the mechanism into its correct position one by one. By manipulating the internal secrets of the lock, the specialist opens it without damage. Depending on the type, quality, and complexity of the lock, this process can take from a few minutes to several hours.

This method is called "manipulative lock opening" or "clean opening." During this process, no part of the lock is damaged, and after it's unlocked by the specialist, it remains fully functional and continues to perform its protective duties.

This process is used when:

  • you've lost or forgotten your keys and don't have access to spares;
  • the door accidentally closed, and the keys are inside the premises;
  • the key's cuts are damaged or broken, and it no longer opens the lock;
  • one of the code heights (the first or last one) on a lever key is worn or broken;
  • the lock stopped opening with the original key, but there are no signs of forceful damage;
  • the key broke partially or completely and is stuck in the lock's keyway.

Upon arrival, our master's primary goal is to open the lock without damage, if the situation allows. To do this, they use specialized tools chosen specifically for your lock's mechanism model.

Masters often open clients' locks using the manipulation method in just a few minutes. It's important to understand that hundreds of hours of painstaking training stand behind these skills and speed, making this work highly skilled and worthy of appropriate compensation.

Opening a door lock without damage allows it to remain fully functional, helping you avoid extra costs for buying a new lock and repairing the door and hardware.

Lock Opening with Damage

Drilling a lock is typically resorted to when its mechanism has partially failed and it's no longer possible to open it without damage using manipulation methods. This happens when a key component inside the lock breaks down — for example, a spring breaks, the cylinder cam wears out, or another issue prevents its normal operation with the original key.

Destructive methods for opening door locks involve the deliberate destruction or damage of their coded elements to unlock the door.

During destructive lock opening, decorative and protective hardware may be damaged, and drill holes might be left in the door overlays.

Drilling locks is one of the fastest ways to open a door, especially when it comes to saving lives or human health (a medical emergency, a child locked inside). In such cases, speed takes precedence over preserving the door and hardware.

Damaging opening is used when:

  • there's an immediate threat to human life and health, and the door needs to be opened instantly;
  • the key is stuck in the lock's cylinder mechanism and the door won't open;
  • the key turns in the cylinder mechanism but the lock bolt doesn't move to the open position;
  • the front door lock was secured from the inside with the night latch;
  • the lock doesn't respond to the key or turn knob because the bolts are completely jammed in the door frame;
  • the cylinder mechanism is partially destroyed after an attempted illegal entry;
  • foreign objects (glue, matches, nails) are in the keyway of the cylinder mechanism and cannot be removed;
  • the lock body was deformed by intruders attempting to pry open the door;
  • currently, there is no mechanical solution to open the lock without damaging its mechanism.

In all these cases, drilling is performed with maximum precision to damage only the necessary elements of the lock, avoiding damage to the door leaf or, at the very least, minimizing it.

Only after the master inspects the locks and explains the problem does the property owner personally give permission to open the doors by damaging the mechanisms.

Remember that destructive opening is a forced measure that ensures the blocked door is opened, but it almost always entails additional costs for replacing locks, and in some cases, for restoring the appearance of the door leaf.

After the door has been opened by drilling, the customer will face mandatory repair costs. The extent of these costs depends on the type of lock and the degree of damage inflicted.
